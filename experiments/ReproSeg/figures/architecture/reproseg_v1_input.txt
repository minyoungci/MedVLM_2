ReproSeg V1: Reproducibility-Equivariant Dual-Stream Brain Segmentation Architecture.
Total parameters: 75.7M (new components: 13.5M = 18%).

The architecture processes a T1-weighted brain MRI volume of shape [B, 1, 192x224x192].

STEP 1 - SHARED STEM:
The input passes through SwinViT Patch Embedding and Stage 0, producing features of shape [B, 48, 96x112x96]. This is where the architecture forks into two parallel streams.

STEP 2 - DUAL STREAMS (running in parallel):

LEFT: ANATOMY STREAM (blue, 62.2M params, BrainSegFounder pretrained):
- Stage 0: 48 channels, spatial 96x112x96
- Stage 1: 96 channels, spatial 48x56x48
- Stage 2: 192 channels, spatial 24x28x24
- Stage 3: 384 channels, spatial 12x14x12
- Bottleneck: 768 channels, spatial 6x7x6
This stream captures anatomical structure, boundaries, and topology.

RIGHT: INVARIANCE STREAM (orange, 5.6M params, random init, half-channel design):
- Stage 0: 24 channels, spatial 96x112x96
- Stage 1: 48 channels, spatial 48x56x48
- Stage 2: 96 channels, spatial 24x28x24
- Stage 3: 192 channels, spatial 12x14x12
This stream captures scanner-specific bias and acquisition noise.
The Stage 3 output feeds into a Gradient Reversal Layer (GRL) followed by a Site Classifier that predicts acquisition site (4 classes: NACC, OASIS, ADNI, AJU). The GRL reverses gradients so the anatomy stream learns to be site-invariant.

STEP 3 - CROSS-STREAM GATING (CSG):
At each of the 4 scale levels, a Cross-Stream Gating module connects the two streams.
The CSG operation is: A_pure = A - sigma(gate) * proj(I)
where gate = sigmoid(Conv1x1([A; proj(I)])), and proj is a 1x1 convolution projecting invariance features to match anatomy channel dimensions.
This structurally subtracts scanner effects from anatomy features. The gate learns how much invariance information to remove at each spatial location.

STEP 4 - SEGMENTATION DECODER (green):
The purified anatomy features (A_pure) are fed as skip connections into the SwinUNETR decoder.
The decoder progressively upsamples from bottleneck back to full resolution.
Output: segmentation logits [B, 12, 192x224x192] for 12 brain tissue classes.

STEP 5 - AUXILIARY OUTPUTS:
- Volume Head (purple, 1.2M params): LR-aware pooling from bottleneck, predicts volumes of 7 dementia-relevant structures (hippocampus L/R, amygdala L/R, entorhinal L/R, ventricle). Output: [B, 7].
- Multi-Scale Feature Consistency (yellow, 5.9M params): For paired scans of the same subject, computes cosine similarity of A_pure features at all 4 encoder scales. This enforces reproducibility at the feature level, not just the output level.

STEP 6 - LOSS FUNCTION:
Four loss terms combined via uncertainty weighting (Kendall et al., CVPR 2018):
L = (1/2*sigma1^2)*L_seg + (1/2*sigma2^2)*L_repro + (1/2*sigma3^2)*L_inv + (1/2*sigma4^2)*L_vol + log(sigma1*sigma2*sigma3*sigma4)
where sigma1-4 are learned parameters. No manual lambda tuning required.
- L_seg: Dice + Cross-Entropy (standard segmentation)
- L_repro: TCL (temporal volume consistency) + multi-scale feature consistency
- L_inv: adversarial site classification via GRL
- L_vol: volume regression (MSE on log1p scale) + seg-volume consistency
